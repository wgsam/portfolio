Smart Reward

Multilevel Reward E-commerce Platform

01. Overview

"Smart Reward" is a multi-level member reward e-commerce platform. It was designed to boost organizational loyalty and user engagement through a unique rewards system, tailored forthe Southeast Asian and Malaysian markets.

02. Business Objectives

Following the overview of 'Smart Reward,' it's essential to outline the core business objectives that guided this project. These goals were established in close collaboration with stakeholders to ensure the platform not only meets but exceeds the strategic needs of the organization.

Methodology:

To accurately capture and align with the business objectives of our stakeholders, we conduct one-on-one interviews with key stakeholder to understand thier vision, priorities and specific goals for this project. We using SWOT analysis to identify the strenght, weakness, oppurtunities, and threts related to the project.

Objectives:

After conducting detailed meetings with stakeholders, we synthesized thier insights and feedback to formulate clear objectives and business goals for this project. This stratergy will drive our development to effectively meet organizational and user need. These objectives include:

Increase Member Engagement:
Drive active participation and continuous interaction with the platform by integrating engaging features. Boost daily active users by 25% within the first year of launch.

Foster Organizational Loyalty:
Build a sense of community and loyalty among members through a tailored shopping expereince that reflects organizational values and culture.

03. User Research

To ensure our design for "Smart Reward", an internal multilevel e-coommerce platform, was user-centric and informed by actual member needs and behavious, we conducted comprehensive user research. This research aimed to understand our users' experiences, preferences, and challenges within a multi-level marketing structure. Here's how we approached this:

Approach:

Our reseach began with identifying key user groups within the organization. These groups included new members, established MLM sellers, and administrative personnel, all of whom interact differently with the platform. We aim to understand their specific challenges, desires, and behaviours to create a tailored shopping and management experience.

Reseach Methodologies and Tools:

We utilized Google Forms to conduct comprehensive surveys. The survey was crafted to guage general user preferences and expectations for a new e-cormmerce platform, specifically focusing on what features they considered most crucial. Question example such as:

"What features are most important to you in an ecommerce platform?"
"Describe a feature you wish to see that would make your shopping experience easier"
"Rank the importance of the following features: Reward System, Product Variety, Payment Option, Dual Wallet"

We distributed this survey via email to all registered members of the organization, achieving a significant engagement with responses from over 200 members.

User Persona

From this rich pool of data, we developed user personas that represented our diverse user base. It's essential to first understand the personas deeply and then translate the needs, behaviors into visualizations.

Research Findings and Conclusions:

The research indicated a strong demand for an intuitive, feature-rich platform that could support a multi-level marketing structure without overwehelming the users.

Key desired features:
  • Streamlined rewards management system
  • Easy-to-navigate interface
  • Dual wallet system
  • Networks Diagram to easily track network growth within the organizatoin
  • Easy tracking referrals and transaction
  • Simplified Checkout Process
Conclusion:

This user research phase was crucial for setting the direction of "Smart Reward". It allowed us to ground our design decisions in actual user needs and preferences, ensuring that the platfrom would resonate well with its intended audience. By linking these insights to user personas, we provided a clear and related context for our design choices, which were crucial for the subsequent phase of the project.

04. User Goals

Following the insights gathered from our comprehensive user research, the design of "Smart Reward" is meticulously focused on achieving specific user goals. These goals are essential for ensuring that the platfrom not only meets but exceeds the expectation of our members, enriching thier interactions with the e-commerce system and fostering increased engagement and satisfaction.

Define User Goals

Through the synthesis of feedback from surveys and members interview, we established several pivotal user goals that guide our core design stratergy:

Simplified Reward and Referral Management
Our users desired a straightforward way to manage thier rewards and referrals. Our response is to design an intuitive dashboard where functionalities related to managing the dual wallet system - seperating cash transactions and reward earnings - are prominently accesible and easy too navigate.
Easy and Clear Navigation
Our goals is to create a user interface that allows members to swiftly locate and manage thier E-wallet and V-Point, alongside other essential features, throuh well-organized menus and consistent, intuive labeling.
Intuitive and Accessible Interface
Catering to our diverse user base, from tech novices to savvy users, it is critical to develop an interface that is universally intuitive. This invlolves simplified interaction designs that accommodate all levels of user experience, ensuring the platform is accessible and straightfoward for every member.
Secure and Flexible Shopping Experience:
Security remains a top priority, especially within the e-commerce landscape. We are dedicated to creating a secure transaction environment where users can feel confident and protected. The dual wallet system enchances this by offering flexible payment option - allowing users to choose between using direct payments or rewards points - thereby enhancing user convenience and satisfacton.
Conclusion:

The user goals for "Smart Reward" serve as a foundational blueprint for the entire project's lifecycle. By aligning our design closely with these goals, we ensure the platform delivers an exceptional user experience, drives robust member engagement, and lays a strong foundaton for ongoing enhancements and user satisfaction.

05. Problem Statements

The development of the "Smart Reward" platform is motivated by a set of critical challenges identified through our user research and initial feedback cycles. The problems impact both the user experience and the operational efficiency of our multi-level marketing e-commerce system. Addressing these issues is paramount to achieving our projects goals and ensuring user satisfaction.

Key Problems:
Complex Reward Management

Problem: Users find the current reward systems on similar platforms overly complex and difficult to understand, especially when managing different types of rewards and transactions. There is a need for a system that simplifies these processes while maintaining transparency and usability.

Impact: This complexity leads to reduced user engagement and satisfaction as member struggle to maximize the benefit of the reward programs

Insufficient Referral Tracking

Problem: Existing MLM platfrom do not provide enough tools for users to effectively track thier referrals and the benefits derived from them. Users require a more intuitive and visual approach to understand thier network's growth and reward structure.

Impact: This complexity leads to reduced user engagement and satisfaction as member struggle to maximize the benefit of the reward programs

Navigation and Usability Issues

Problem: Users often experience frustration due to poor navigation and interface design, which make it difficult to find necessary information and complete transaction smoothly.

Impact: Poor usability can lead to increased user errors, decreased satisficatioon, and higher abandonment rates, affecting the overall success of the platform.

Limited Payment Flexibility

Problem: Current platfrom often offer limited payment options, which do not cater to the diverse preferences of a broad user base, including different financial situations and access to payment methods.

Impact: This limitation can prevent potential user from fully engaging with the platform, thus limiting market expansion and user inclusivity.

Conclusion:

Each problem statement has been carefully derived from user input and market analysis to ensure that our solutions are directly addressing the real challenges faced by our users. In the subsequent sections, we will explore how our design proposals specifically tackle these issues to create a more user-friendly e-commerce experience.
